UNDERWATER ROV

Overview

This project was developed for the Western Engineering Competition (WEC) in the Re-Engineering category. Our team was given a problem statement to convert a terrestrial RC vehicle into a functional underwater ROV. We successfully completed our design, report, and presentation within an allotted 6-hour time frame, earning us 1st Place in our category and qualifying to represent Western University at the provincial level during the Ontario Engineering Competition (OEC).

Re-Engineered Design Considerations:

  • Aluminum Support Frame: Designed with protective guards and pool-noodle bumpers to provide high structural rigidity and impact resistance for internal electronics. Materials selected are cost-efficient, environmentally safe, and widely available.
  • Engineered Thrusters: Waterproof motor housings featuring front-facing intake ducts and O-ring seals to improve laminar flow and maximize propulsion efficiency.
  • Piston-Style Ballast Tank: Driven by a linear actuator to control displacement, allowing the ROV to achieve precise buoyancy and vertical depth control.

Decision Process & Report

  • Conducted a structured selection process using Go/No-Go screenings and Weighted Decision Matrices to identify the Tamiya Super Clod Buster as the optimal RC vehicle for ROV conversion.
  • Performed a sensitivity analysis on design objectives (durability, modifiability, and volume) to validate vehicle selection against project constraints.
  • Managed project timelines and documentation using Gantt charts to ensure all design phases were completed within competition deadlines.

Main Contributions (Group Size: 4)

  • Group Management
  • CAD Design for metal frame
  • Decision Processes & Report-Writing

SPACE TELESCOPE

Overview

As provincial finalists at the Ontario Engineering Competition, our team was tasked with re-engineering critical components of the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST). We focused our modifications on the Spacecraft Bus across two separate challenges:

  • Case 1 (Structural & Systems): A week-long intensive research and design project focused on increasing the spacecraft's operational lifespan.
  • Case 2 (Communications): A 6-hour project to re-engineer signal transmission architecture for deep-space suitability.

Case 1 Modifications

  • Material Optimization: Replaced Aluminum Honeycomb panels with High-Modulus E-CFRP (Epoxy Adhesive Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer) to reduce overall spacecraft mass.
  • Hybrid Thruster System: Replaced 8 of 12 chemical thrusters with MaSMi Hall Effect thrusters, vastly improving fuel efficiency and operational lifespan.
  • Power Integration: Swapped traditional solar arrays for a small-scale fission reactor system to provide a power source suitable for deep-space.

Methods & Analysis

Created a modified optimization equation to understand the relationship between dry mass, propellant mass, thruster specific impulse, and longevity of telescope. This formula aided in selecting components with appropriate specifications.

Utilized Ashby charts for materials selection and conducted extensive degradation mechanism analysis. Our iterations were capable of extending the JWST's lifespan by 300%.

Case 2 Modifications

  • Laser Transmission Network: Developed an intermediate satellite constellation utilizing laser communication to increase transmission rates and weaken attenuation over long distances.
  • Redundant Pathing & Risk Mitigation: Designed a multipath transmission relay system to mitigate signal interference from celestial bodies.
  • Targeted Subsystem Optimization: Each intermediate satellite optimized based on its distance from the sun to maintain power requirements in varying solar flux environments.

Case 1:

MAGLIN ICONIC BENCH

Overview

In collaboration with Maglin Site Furniture, our group engineered a sustainable solution for the 'Iconic Bench' following the endangerment of their primary material, Ipe wood. Through in-depth indexing and manufacturing analysis, we proposed Black Locust as a high-performance, wood-based replacement. Our design ensures long-term outdoor durability while adhering to modern environmental compliance and ethical sourcing standards.

Methods

  • Utilized a systematic screening process, including Go/No-Go charts and Pugh matrices, to identify a sustainable alternative to endangered Ipe wood.
  • Used Ashby Charts to compare Young's Modulus, yield strength, and density across various North American hardwoods and engineered lumbers.
  • Selected Thermally Modified Black Locust due to its high Janka hardness, exceptional decay resistance, and 50-year service life without chemical preservatives.
  • Tested for failure modes such as thermal cycling, distributed loads from heavy snowfall, and bending using SolidWorks FEA Simulations.

Main Contributions (Group Size: 4)

  • Finite Element Analysis Testing
  • Materials Selection Research
  • Final Report Writing

AQUAPONIC FARM

Overview

As a member of Engineers Without Borders (Western Chapter), I am part of a multi-disciplinary team designing and building a sustainable Aquaponic Farm. Since September 2024, we have progressed through several design iterations and are currently in the active fabrication phase, moving from digital twin concepts to a physical, working system.

Proposal & Goals

Guided by the core mission of Engineers Without Borders, this project focuses on food security and sustainable infrastructure. Our primary goal was to design an accessible, low-cost aquaponic system that could be constructed using standard workshop tools and readily available materials like PVC and pine. This project serves as a practical opportunity to mentor engineers in hands-on fabrication.

Current & Future Steps

  • Fish Enrichment & Structural Design: Designed and fabricated a custom "fish tunnel" to improve the tank's internal architecture. Modeled in SolidWorks and integrated into the physical tank.
  • Future Iterations: Developing a real-time monitoring system using LabVIEW to track pH levels, water temperature, and nitrate concentrations. Creating long-term maintenance plans.

Main Contributions (Group Size: 6)

  • Woodworking
  • Research on plants, fish, and hydrostatics
  • Materials Selection
  • CAD Design & Prototyping (Wooden frame and fish tunnel)
